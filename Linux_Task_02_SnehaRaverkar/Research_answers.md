# Part G:-

## 1. Why are file permissions important?
Ans:File permissions are important because they help protect files from unauthorized access. They decide who can read, write, or execute a file. This helps keep important information safe from misuse. Permissions also prevent users from accidentally changing or deleting files. In multi-user systems, they help maintain privacy and security. Proper permissions make the system more organized and secure

## 2. What happens if sensitive files are given 777 permissions?
Ans:Permission 777 gives full access to everyone on the system. Any user can read, modify, delete, or execute the file. If the file contains sensitive information, it can be viewed or changed by unauthorized users. This increases the risk of data theft and security breaches. Important files may also be deleted accidentally. Therefore, 777 permissions should be avoided for sensitive files.

## 3. What is the Principle of Least Privilege?
Ans: The Principle of Least Privilege means giving users only the access they need to do their work. They should not get extra permissions. This reduces security risks and helps protect the system.

## 4. Why do organizations restrict user access?
Ans: Organizations restrict user access to protect their data and systems. Different employees have different responsibilities, so they do not need access to everything. Limiting access helps prevent accidental or intentional misuse of information. It also reduces the risk of data leaks and cyberattacks. Access control helps maintain confidentiality and system security. This ensures that important resources are available only to authorized users.
