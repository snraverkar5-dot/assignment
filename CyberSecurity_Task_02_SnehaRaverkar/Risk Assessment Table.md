
# Risk Assessment for a College

## Risk Assessment Table

| Asset | Threat | Impact | Risk Level |
|---------|---------|---------|------------|
| College Website | Website Defacement or DDoS Attack | Website may become unavailable or show incorrect information | Medium |
| Financial Records | Data Theft or Ransomware | Financial information may be lost or stolen | High |
| Computer Lab Systems | Malware Infection | Systems may become slow, damaged, or unusable | Medium |
| Student Records | Data Breach or Unauthorized Access | Personal information may be stolen or modified | High |
| Attendance Database | Unauthorized Modification | Attendance records may become inaccurate | Medium |
| Network Infrastructure | Malware or Hacking Attack | Network services may stop working and affect college operations | High |
| Faculty Information | Phishing or Data Theft | Faculty personal and professional information may be exposed | High |
| Library Management System | Unauthorized Access | Library records may be modified or deleted | Medium |
| Examination Data | Data Tampering | Exam papers or results may be changed | High |
| Student Email Accounts | Phishing Attack | Student accounts may be compromised and misused | High |

## Conclusion

A college has many important assets that need protection from cyber threats. If these assets are attacked, the college may lose important data, services, and trust. Using strong passwords, security updates, backups, and cybersecurity awareness can help keep these assets safe.
