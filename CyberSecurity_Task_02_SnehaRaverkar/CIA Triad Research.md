# Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad)

## 1. Confidentiality

### Definition
Confidentiality means keeping information secret and allowing only authorized people to see it.

### Why it is Important
It helps protect personal and private information from people who should not access it.

### Real-World Example
A password is used to protect a Gmail account so that only the account owner can read the emails.

---

## 2. Integrity

### Definition
Integrity means keeping information correct and unchanged.

### Why it is Important
It makes sure that data is accurate and nobody changes it without permission.

### Real-World Example
A student's marks should stay correct in the school database and should not be changed by anyone else.

---

## 3. Availability

### Definition
Availability means information and systems should be available whenever people need them.

### Why it is Important
Users must be able to access data and services without problems.

### Real-World Example
An online banking website should work whenever customers want to check their account balance.

---

# Comparison Table

| Concept | Definition | Why it is Important | Real-World Example |
|----------|------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Confidentiality | Keeping information secret from unauthorized people. | Protects private and sensitive information. | Password-protected Gmail account |
| Integrity | Keeping information accurate and unchanged. | Ensures data remains correct and trustworthy. | Student marks stored correctly in a database |
| Availability | Making sure information and services are accessible when needed. | Allows users to access systems anytime. | Online banking website working for customers |
