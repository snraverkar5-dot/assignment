# Report: Improving Cybersecurity Posture of the College

## Introduction

Cybersecurity is important for protecting the college's data, systems, and network infrastructure. As educational institutions store sensitive information such as student records, examination data, and financial information, they must take proper security measures to reduce cyber risks.

## Top 5 Risks

### 1. Phishing Attacks

Students and staff may receive fake emails designed to steal usernames, passwords, or other sensitive information.

### 2. Malware Infections

Viruses, ransomware, and other malware can damage systems, steal data, and disrupt college operations.

### 3. Weak Passwords

Weak or reused passwords can allow attackers to gain unauthorized access to accounts and systems.

### 4. Unauthorized Access

Improper access controls may allow unauthorized users to view, modify, or delete sensitive information.

### 5. Unpatched Systems

Outdated software and operating systems may contain vulnerabilities that attackers can exploit.

## Top 5 Recommendations

### 1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Require an additional verification step for user accounts to improve account security.

### 2. Conduct Regular Security Awareness Training

Educate students and staff about phishing, social engineering, and safe online practices.

### 3. Apply Security Updates and Patches

Keep all software, operating systems, and applications updated to reduce vulnerabilities.

### 4. Enforce Strong Password Policies

Require complex passwords and encourage regular password changes.

### 5. Perform Regular Data Backups

Maintain secure backups of important data to support recovery after cyber incidents.

## Expected Benefits

* Improved protection of student and faculty information.
* Reduced risk of cyber attacks and data breaches.
* Better availability and reliability of college systems.
* Faster recovery from security incidents and data loss.
* Increased awareness of cybersecurity threats among users.
* Enhanced trust and reputation of the institution.

## Conclusion

By addressing key cybersecurity risks and implementing appropriate security controls, the college can significantly improve its cybersecurity posture. Regular monitoring, user awareness, and proactive security measures will help protect critical assets and ensure a secure learning environment.
