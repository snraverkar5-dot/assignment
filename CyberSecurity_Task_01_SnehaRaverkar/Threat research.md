# Common Cyber Security Threats

## 1. Phishing

### Definition

Phishing is when a bad person sends a fake email, message, or website to trick people into giving their passwords or personal information.

### Real-World Example

You get an email saying you won a prize and it asks for your bank details. The email is fake and made to steal your information.

### Prevention Methods

* Do not click unknown links.
* Check the sender's email address.
* Do not share passwords with anyone.
* Ask an adult or teacher if you are unsure.

---

## 2. Malware

### Definition

Malware is harmful software that can damage a computer or steal information.

### Real-World Example

You download a free game from an unknown website, and it installs a virus on your computer.

### Prevention Methods

* Use antivirus software.
* Download files only from trusted websites.
* Keep your computer updated.
* Do not open suspicious files.

---

## 3. Ransomware

### Definition

Ransomware is a type of malware that locks your files and asks for money to unlock them.

### Real-World Example

A person's photos and documents become locked, and a message appears asking for money to get them back.

### Prevention Methods

* Keep backups of important files.
* Do not open unknown attachments.
* Use antivirus software.
* Keep software updated.

---

## 4. Social Engineering

### Definition

Social engineering is when someone tricks people into giving secret information.

### Real-World Example

A stranger calls and pretends to be from a bank and asks for your password or OTP.

### Prevention Methods

* Never share passwords or OTPs.
* Verify who is asking for information.
* Be careful with phone calls and messages.
* Ask a trusted person if you are unsure.

---

## 5. Data Breach

### Definition

A data breach happens when private information is stolen or seen by people who should not have access to it.

### Real-World Example

A company gets hacked and customer names, emails, and passwords are leaked.

### Prevention Methods

* Use strong passwords.
* Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
* Keep software updated.
* Do not share personal information unnecessarily.

---

## Conclusion

These cyber threats can harm computers and steal important information. By using strong passwords, being careful online, and keeping devices updated, we can stay safer on the internet.
